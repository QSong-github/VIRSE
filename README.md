# Background
The structural heterogeneity of RNA molecules, especially in the presence of dynamic modifications such as m6A, poses a significant challenge for traditional single-structure prediction methods. While SHAPE-MaP provides experimental constraints on RNA structure, existing computational approaches differ in how they handle RNA conformational ensembles. RNAfold2, which relies on thermodynamic energy parameters, can predict RNA structures and explore alternative conformations through sampling-based methods, but it does not explicitly model the probabilistic distribution of coexisting conformations within a heterogeneous population. To address these challenges, we develop VARISEM, a Variational Bayesian clustering framework, to infer RNA structural ensembles from high-throughput SHAPE-MaP1 and eTAM-seq4 data. By integrating structural and modification-specific signals at the single-molecule level, VARISEM will enable a quantitative assessment of how m6A modifications reshape the structural landscape of 7SK RNA, providing insights into their regulatory role in NSCLC progression.
